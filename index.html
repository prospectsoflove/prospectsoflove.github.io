Description


The project frames the anxiety and cultural ambiguity which surrounds the Antarctic continent through a radical contemporary domestic context using the narrative format of a 'telenovela' – removing it from the explicit science and political medias that frame it now.  Prospects of Love looks at the Antarctic continent as a potential flashpoint for conflict over mineral and biological resources, in light of the expiration of the Antarctic Treaty System in 2048, weighing the risks and benefits of opening up the region to exploitation in response to current and future issues.

Set in Miami, the plot revolves around two lovers: Maria Bellinghaussen, an environmental activist and Arturo Prat, a young successful Bio-prospector. Their love, however, is impossible because of ideological differences and by the fact that Maria's father, Klaus Bellinghaussen is Arturo's arch nemesis in the field of Bio-prospecting, the plot thickens...


Prospects of Love was produced for the first Antarctic Biennale 2017 and exhibited in the Antarctic Pavilion as part of the 57th Venice Biennale.

Biography


Graham Baldwin and Stefan laxness are an American-Icelandic duo based in London who both graduated from the Architectural Association (AA) in London.

During their time at the AA, Graham co-founded a notable publication titles 'Fulcrum, Commonplace' featured at the 14th Venice Biennale, while Stefan received the KPF travel scholarship to produce a video essay about the changing landscape of China's borderlands and that of its Central Asian neighbours. Since graduating, Graham places as a runner up in a UNESCO competition to design a new cultural centre in Bamiyan, Afghanistan, whilst working as an architect for Carmody Groake. Stefan, is currently an architectural researcher at Forensic Architecture (FA), a London based research agency that focuses on conflict through gathering and presenting spatial analysis in legal and political forum. Since joining FA, Stefan has worked on developing methods to analyse and model airstrikes in the Middle East, models of which were exhibited at the 15th Venice Biennale in the V&A’s ‘A World Of Fragile Parts’; and has worked extensively on FA's latest project in collaboration with Amnesty International 'Saydnaya: Inside A Syrian Torture Prison'. A project in which new witness testimony was generated by directly engaging ex-detainees with architecture and sound modelling techniques in order to shed light on the brutal inner workings of Saydnaya prison.
